Phishing Prevention Center

1

Inspect the Address Bar First

Attackers use visual similarity (lookalike domains) such as paypa1.com or paypal-security.com. Check the exact domain before writing anything.

2

Look for Unencrypted Sites (HTTP)

Legitimate login portals are almost exclusively secured via HTTPS. If the connection says "Not Secure", do not submit sensitive credentials.

3

Credential Harvesting Forms

Phishing templates are designed to submit your forms directly back to the attacker's server, which may be located under completely different domains.

4

Verify Through Official Channels

If a bank or provider alerts you about "suspended access" with a button to click, close the link and navigate to your official banking app directly.
